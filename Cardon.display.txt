The forested mountains above Lake Chapala, the Sierra de San Juan Cosalá, 
are part of the Bajío Dry Forests ecoregion of west central Mexico. 
Due to agricultural, residential, and industrial development in the Bajío, 
only about 5% of the original habitat remains. 
So the Sierra de San Juan Cosalá is a very significant remnant of 
original forest that remains largely undeveloped, 
and is at least partially protected.
